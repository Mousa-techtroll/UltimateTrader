## Overview

Every signal generated by UltimateTrader passes through an **8-step risk pipeline** before execution. Each step can only **reduce** position size from the base---no step inflates risk. The pipeline guarantees that the final risk per trade is always bounded by a hard cap, regardless of how optimistic the underlying signal is.

## Step 1: Quality Tier Base Risk

The entry point of the risk pipeline. The system scores every signal on a 0-10 point scale, maps that score to a quality tier, and assigns a base risk percentage.

### Quality Scoring Factors

| Factor | Points | How It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Trend Alignment** | 0--3 | D1 == H4 and both directional: +2. D1 neutral but H4 directional: +1. D1/H4 aligned from context: +1 bonus. Pattern direction matches H4: +1. |
| **CHoCH (Change of Character)** | 0--2 | Mutually exclusive with trend alignment. If a recent CHoCH is detected and aligns with pattern direction, the system takes whichever score is higher---trend alignment OR CHoCH (+2). They do not stack. |
| **RSI Extreme** | +3 | Awarded when RSI > overbought threshold or RSI < oversold threshold. A flat bonus that rewards counter-trend setups at extremes. |
| **Regime** | 0--2 | TRENDING: +2. VOLATILE: +1. RANGING: +1. CHOPPY: +0. UNKNOWN with D1==H4 alignment: +1. |
| **Macro Alignment** | 0--3 | abs(macro_score) >= 3: +3. abs(macro_score) >= 1: +1. Neutral (macro_score == 0): +1 (fallback). |
| **Pattern Quality** | 0--2 | LiquiditySweep, Displacement, BB Mean Reversion, Range Box, Volatility Breakout, OB Retest, FVG Mitigation, Silver Bullet, Compression, Institutional Candle, Panic Momentum: +2. Engulfing, Pin Bar, MA Cross, SFP, London Close Rev: +1. |

**Engine confluence bonus:** When a signal originates from an engine (Liquidity, Session, or Expansion) with `engine_confluence >= 70`, the evaluator adds +1 point to the total score.

**Bear regime shift:** In bear regime, SHORT signals from BB Mean Reversion are force-upgraded to SETUP_A (skipping normal scoring). MA Cross shorts are force-upgraded to SETUP_B_PLUS. All other bear-regime shorts receive +2 bonus points.

**Cap:** The total score is capped at **10 points**, regardless of how many bonuses accumulate.

### Tier Mapping

| Quality Tier | Required Points | Base Risk % | Default Input |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A+** | >= 8 | 0.8% | `InpRiskAPlusSetup` (equalized from 1.0% — A+ had PF 1.00 at 1.0%, oversized) |
| **A** | >= 7 | 0.8% | `InpRiskASetup` |
| **B+** | >= 6 | 0.6% | `InpRiskBPlusSetup` |
| **B** | >= 5 | 0.5% | `InpRiskBSetup` |
| **None** | < 5 | Rejected | Signal not traded |

The thresholds are configurable via Group 22 inputs (`InpPointsAPlusSetup`, `InpPointsASetup`, `InpPointsBPlusSetup`, `InpPointsBSetup`).

## Step 2: Consecutive Loss Scaling

After determining base risk, the pipeline applies a drawdown reduction based on the current losing streak.

| Consecutive Losses | Multiplier | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0--1 | 1.00x | No change |
| 2--3 | 0.75x | Risk reduced by 25% |
| 4+ | 0.50x | Risk reduced by 50% |

**Tracking:** The `CQualityTierRiskStrategy` class maintains `m_consecutive_losses` and `m_consecutive_wins` counters. A win resets the loss counter to zero and vice versa. The counters are updated by `RecordTradeResult()` after each trade closes.

**Toggle:** `InpEnableLossScaling` (Group 4, default `true`). Multipliers are configurable via `InpLossLevel1Reduction` and `InpLossLevel2Reduction`.

---

## Step 3: Volatility Regime Adjustment

The `CVolatilityRegimeManager` classifies current volatility into one of five tiers by comparing the current H1 ATR to a 120-bar rolling average.

### Volatility Tiers

| Tier | ATR Ratio | Risk Multiplier | SL Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VOL_VERY_LOW** | < 0.5x avg | 1.00x | None |
| **VOL_LOW** | 0.5--0.7x avg | 0.92x | None |
| **VOL_NORMAL** | 0.7--1.0x avg | 1.00x | None |
| **VOL_HIGH** | 1.0--1.3x avg | 0.85x | SL tightened to 0.85x ATR multiplier |
| **VOL_EXTREME** | > 1.3x avg | 0.65x | SL tightened to 0.70x ATR multiplier |

The risk multiplier is applied directly to the running risk percentage. SL tightening is a separate mechanism that reduces the ATR-based stop loss distance (not the risk percentage itself), resulting in a smaller stop and therefore slightly larger position size for the same dollar risk---but the dollar risk is already reduced by 0.85x or 0.65x, so net exposure always shrinks.

**Toggle:** `InpEnableVolRegime` (Group 14, default `true`). All thresholds and multipliers are configurable in Group 14.

## Step 4: Short Protection (3 Tiers)

Short positions in a gold-focused EA carry structural risk (gold's long-term bullish bias). The pipeline applies a tiered reduction to all shorts, with exemptions for specific pattern types.

| Short Category | Multiplier | Pattern Types |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard shorts** | 0.50x | Engulfing, Pin Bar, MA Cross, Displacement, any trend-following short |
| **Mean reversion shorts** | 0.70x | BB Mean Reversion, Range Box, False Breakout Fade |
| **Exempt shorts** | 1.00x (no reduction) | Volatility Breakout, Crash Breakout |

The standard multiplier is configurable via `InpShortRiskMultiplier` (Group 3, default `0.5`).

**Rationale:** Breakout and crash shorts occur during momentum-driven moves where the bearish thesis is strongest. Mean reversion shorts have a structural edge in ranging markets and warrant less penalty. Standard trend-following shorts against gold's inherent bullish drift receive the full 50% reduction.

## Step 5: Health-Based Adjustment

The `CHealthBasedRiskAdjuster` provides a real-time system health multiplier via the `IMarketContext.GetHealthRiskAdjustment()` interface. This multiplier reflects the overall operational health of the EA infrastructure:

- **HEALTH_EXCELLENT / HEALTH_GOOD:** 1.0x (no reduction)
- **HEALTH_FAIR:** ~0.85x
- **HEALTH_DEGRADED:** ~0.6x
- **HEALTH_CRITICAL:** ~0.3x (near-halt)

Health status is determined by the `HealthMonitor`, which tracks:
- Indicator handle validity
- Trade execution success rate
- Order modification failure rate
- Timeout detection frequency
- Memory and error states

**Toggle:** `InpUseHealthBasedRisk` (Group 24, default `true`).

## Step 6: Engine Weight

Each engine (Liquidity, Session, Expansion) and each legacy entry plugin maintains a dynamic performance weight. The weight is a composite score computed from four factors:

### Composite Formula

```
Weight = (0.40 x PF_Score) + (0.30 x Stability) + (0.20 x MAE_Efficiency) + (0.10 x Expectancy_Score)
```

| Component | Weight | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit Factor (PF)** | 40% | Piecewise: PF < 1.0 maps to 0.0--0.3, PF 1.0--1.5 maps to 0.3--0.7, PF > 1.5 maps to 0.7--1.0 |
| **Stability (R-value std dev)** | 30% | `1.0 - (stddev(R) / 3.0)`, clamped 0--1. Low variance in trade outcomes = high stability. |
| **MAE Efficiency** | 20% | `1.0 - (avg_MAE / avg_MFE)`, clamped 0--1. Measures how little adverse excursion trades experience relative to their favorable excursion. |
| **Expectancy** | 10% | Normalized expectancy per trade. |

**Drawdown penalty:** If the engine's current drawdown from its peak cumulative profit exceeds a threshold, up to 15% is subtracted from the composite weight.

**Clamping:** The final weight is clamped to the range **[0.30, 1.00]**. A weight of 0.30 means the engine's risk is reduced to 30% of what the previous steps calculated. A weight of 1.00 means no reduction.

**Key constraint:** The engine weight is applied via `MathMin(1.0, engine_weight)`, ensuring it can **only reduce risk, never inflate it**. If the engine has no performance data yet, the weight defaults to 1.0 (no penalty).

## Step 7: Session Execution Quality

The execution quality gate prevents trades during adverse market microstructure conditions. It produces a score from 0.0 to 1.0 composed of three sub-scores:

### 3-Component Calculation

| Component | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Historical execution quality** | 50% | Average slippage + spread cost relative to normal. Computed per session (Asia, London, NY) from `ExecutionMetrics`. |
| **Spread stability** | 25% | Detects spread spikes. Current spread compared to rolling baseline. A spike ratio > 2.0 degrades this component toward 0. |
| **Tick activity** | 25% | Dead market detection. If tick count over the last N seconds is below threshold, this component approaches 0. |

### Gating Thresholds

| Quality Score | Action |
|---|---|
| **< 0.25** | **Block entry entirely.** No trades are opened. |
| **0.25 -- 0.50** | **Halve risk.** Risk percentage is multiplied by 0.50. |
| **> 0.50** | No additional reduction. |

**Toggle:** `InpEnableSessionQualityGate` (Group 36, default `true`). Thresholds are configurable via `InpExecQualityBlockThresh` (default `0.25`) and `InpExecQualityReduceThresh` (default `0.50`).

---

## Step 8: Hard Cap

The final step enforces an absolute ceiling. Regardless of all prior calculations, risk per trade is capped at:

```
effective_max = min(caller_max_risk, InpMaxRiskPerTrade)
```

| Parameter | Default | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `InpMaxRiskPerTrade` | 1.2% | Hard cap per trade |
| Minimum floor | 0.1% | Prevents rounding to zero |

After capping, the pipeline calculates lot size from account balance, risk amount, and stop distance, then normalizes to broker specifications (lot step, min/max lot, margin check).

## Worked Example

**Scenario:** A Bullish Engulfing signal on XAUUSD during London session.

### Signal Context
- D1 trend: Bullish. H4 trend: Bullish.
- Regime: TRENDING. ADX = 28.
- Macro score: +2 (DXY falling, VIX low).
- RSI: 45 (not extreme).
- No recent CHoCH.
- Account balance: $10,000.
- Entry: 2350.00. Stop loss: 2340.00 (1000 points, ATR-based).
- 1 consecutive loss on record.
- Current ATR/avg ratio: 1.15 (VOL_HIGH).
- System health: EXCELLENT (1.0x).
- Engine weight: 0.82 (Liquidity engine, 35 trades, PF=1.4, stability=0.7).
- Session quality: 0.68 (normal London execution).

### Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Quality Tier Base Risk**
```
Trend alignment:     D1 == H4 == Bullish       = +2
                     D1/H4 aligned from context = +1
                     Pattern (Bullish) matches H4 = +1
CHoCH:               None (trend points kept)
RSI Extreme:         RSI 45, not extreme        = +0
Regime:              TRENDING                    = +2
Macro alignment:     abs(+2) >= 1               = +1
Pattern quality:     Engulfing                   = +1
---------------------------------------------------
Total:               8 points -> A+ tier
Base risk:           1.0%
```

**Step 2: Consecutive Loss Scaling**
```
1 consecutive loss: below threshold (2).
Multiplier: 1.00x
Risk: 1.0% x 1.00 = 1.0%
```

**Step 3: Volatility Regime Adjustment**
```
ATR ratio 1.15 -> VOL_HIGH tier
Multiplier: 0.85x
Risk: 1.0% x 0.85 = 0.85%
(SL also tightened to 0.85x ATR multiplier, but this affects stop distance, not risk %)
```

**Step 4: Short Protection**
```
Signal is BUY. No short protection applied.
Multiplier: 1.00x
Risk: 0.85%
```

**Step 5: Health-Based Adjustment**
```
Health status: EXCELLENT
Multiplier: 1.00x
Risk: 0.85%
```

**Step 6: Engine Weight**
```
Engine weight: 0.82
Risk: 0.85% x min(1.0, 0.82) = 0.85% x 0.82 = 0.697%
```

**Step 7: Session Execution Quality**
```
Quality score: 0.68 (above 0.50 threshold)
No additional reduction.
Risk: 0.697%
```

**Step 8: Hard Cap**
```
0.697% < 1.2% cap -> no capping needed
0.697% > 0.1% floor -> no floor needed
Final risk: 0.697%
```

### Lot Calculation
```
Risk amount: $10,000 x 0.697% = $69.70
Stop distance: 1000 points
Tick value (XAUUSD, 1 lot): ~$0.01/point = $10/1000 points
Lots = $69.70 / (1000 x $0.01) = 0.07 lots (normalized to broker step)
```

## Supplementary Risk Controls

### Shock Volatility Override

An intra-bar circuit breaker that operates **outside** the 8-step pipeline. It fires before a signal even enters risk calculation.

**Detection (3 checks, any one triggers):**

| Check | Condition | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| H1 bar range vs ATR | `bar_range > InpShockBarRangeThresh x ATR` (default 2.0x) | Shock |
| Spread spike | Current spread > 3.0x rolling average | Extreme |
| M5 range | M5 bar range > 0.8x H1 ATR | Extreme |

If `bar_range > 1.5 x threshold` OR spread > 3.0x OR M5 range > 0.8x ATR, the condition is classified as **extreme shock** and all entries are blocked until the condition clears.

**Toggle:** `InpEnableShockDetection` (Group 38, default `true`).

---

### Per-Strategy Auto-Kill Gate

The auto-kill system monitors every entry plugin's forward performance and disables strategies that are losing money.

**Evaluation tiers:**

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Early kill** | 10+ trades AND PF < 0.8 | Strategy disabled immediately |
| **Standard kill** | 20+ trades AND PF < 1.1 | Strategy disabled |

When a strategy is auto-killed:
1. Its `auto_disabled` flag is set to `true`.
2. All future signals from that plugin are skipped in `CSignalOrchestrator::ScanForSignals()`.
3. The kill is logged with timestamp and performance metrics.
4. The strategy can be re-enabled programmatically via `ReEnablePlugin()` or by resetting via `ResetPluginPerformance()`.

**PF sentinel:** A PF of 99.0 (meaning zero losses / infinite PF) is never killed---it indicates a perfect record.

**Toggle:** `InpDisableAutoKill` (Group 28, default `false` = auto-kill is enabled).

---

### Per-Mode Auto-Kill Gate

Separate from per-strategy auto-kill. Each engine mode (OB Retest, FVG Mitigation, SFP, Silver Bullet, London Close, Compression BO, Institutional Candle) tracks its own `ModePerformance` struct.

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **PF gate** | `InpModeKillMinTrades` (default 15) trades AND PF < `InpModeKillPFThreshold` (default 0.9) | Mode disabled |
| **PF at maturity** | 30 trades AND PF < 1.1 | Mode disabled |
| **Negative expectancy** | 40 trades AND expectancy < 0 | Mode disabled |

Mode performance is persisted to the state file and survives EA restarts.

### Daily Limits

| Limit | Default | Input Parameter | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily loss halt** | 3.0% | `InpDailyLossLimit` | Trading halted for the remainder of the day. Resets at midnight server time. |
| **Max concurrent positions** | 5 | `InpMaxPositions` | New entries rejected if already at limit. |
| **Max trades per day** | 5 | `InpMaxTradesPerDay` | New entries rejected after limit reached. Resets daily. |

| **Max margin usage** | 80.0% | `InpMaxMarginUsage` | Margin check at lot calculation rejects if margin required > 80% of free margin. |

### Counter-Trend Risk Reduction

When `InpUseDaily200EMA` is enabled (Group 19, default `true`), the `CSignalValidator` applies the D1 200 EMA filter as a **binary gate** with exceptions, not as a multiplier:

**Price above D1 200 EMA (bull market):**
- Shorts are **rejected** unless they meet one of the explicit exceptions:
  - Breakout short with H4 bearish + ADX >= 26
  - Strong ADX (> validation threshold) with H4 bearish and macro bearish
  - Mean reversion short with low ADX and bearish macro/H4
  - Asia session MR short with extreme overbought RSI

**Price below D1 200 EMA (bear market):**
- Longs are rejected unless:
  - H4 is bullish, RSI is extremely oversold, or MR pattern with positive macro
  - Asia session exception
  - Strongly bullish macro score

When a counter-trend trade is allowed through the exception logic, the short protection multiplier (Step 4) still applies at 0.50x, effectively cutting risk by 50% compared to the same trade taken with-trend.

## Session Risk Controls

Per-session risk multipliers adjust position sizing based on observed session-level performance characteristics.

| Session | Risk Multiplier | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **London** | 0.50x | Higher chop and false breakout frequency during London session. |
| **NY** | 0.90x | Slightly elevated noise during NY overlap period. |
| **Asia** | 1.00x | Unchanged; lower volatility with cleaner setups. |

The session multiplier is applied as an additional step in the risk pipeline, reducing the effective risk percentage for trades entered during London and NY sessions.

---

## Entry Sanity Gate

A pre-execution sanity check rejects trades where the stop loss distance is too small relative to the current spread:

**Rule:** Reject entry if `SL distance < 3 x current spread`.

This prevents opening trades where spread alone could consume a significant portion of the risk budget, which would result in poor fill quality and distorted R-multiples.

---

## Early Invalidation

A post-entry safety mechanism that closes non-performing positions early, before they reach full stop loss.

**Conditions (all must be true):**
- Within the first **3 bars** of the trade
- MFE_R <= 0.20 (trade has barely moved in the favorable direction)
- MAE_R >= 0.40 (trade has already moved 40% toward stop loss)
- TP0 has **not** been captured

**Safety rules:**
- Never triggers after TP0, TP1, TP2, or trailing stage
- Only fires during the initial stage of the position lifecycle

This mechanism cuts losers faster when the trade shows no directional intent, reducing the average loss on failed setups.

## Risk Pipeline Diagram

```
Signal Detected
     |
     v
[Step 1] Quality Tier  -->  Base risk % (0.5% -- 1.0%)
     |
     v
[Step 2] Loss Scaling   -->  x 0.50 -- 1.00
     |
     v
[Step 3] Vol Regime     -->  x 0.65 -- 1.00
     |
     v
[Step 4] Short Protect  -->  x 0.50 -- 1.00
     |
     v
[Step 5] Health Adjust  -->  x 0.30 -- 1.00
     |
     v
[Step 6] Engine Weight  -->  x 0.30 -- 1.00
     |
     v
[Step 7] Session Quality --> Block / Halve / Pass
     |
     v
[Step 8] Hard Cap       -->  max 1.2%, min 0.1%
     |
     v
Lot Size Calculation --> Broker Normalization --> Trade Execution
```

**Theoretical minimum risk:** 0.1% floor (Step 8).
**Theoretical maximum risk:** 1.2% cap (Step 8).
**Worst-case stacking:** 1.0% x 0.50 x 0.65 x 0.50 x 0.30 x 0.30 x 0.50 = 0.0073% -- floored to 0.1%.
